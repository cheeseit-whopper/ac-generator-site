AC Generator

Introduction

An AC generator, also known as an alternator, converts mechanical energy into electrical energy using electromagnetic induction.

Working Principle

As the coil rotates within a magnetic field, the magnetic flux linkage changes, inducing an electromotive force (EMF) and generating alternating current (AC).

Parts of an AC Generator

  • Field: Produces the magnetic field.
  • Armature: The rotating coil where EMF is induced.
  • Slip Rings: Provide a continuous connection between the rotating coil and external circuit.
  • Brushes: Maintain electrical contact with the slip rings.

Applications

AC generators are widely used in power plants, vehicles, and portable generators to supply electricity for various needs.
